## CREATIVE DIRECTION (interpret freely, make bold choices)

The board currently looks like competent generic SaaS. It needs personality. Here is the design direction and intent — execute it however you see fit as long as it serves these principles:

### The Problem to Solve
Every surface is the same flat white-gray. There is no focal point, no dominant visual element, no sense of depth beyond hairline borders. The brand color barely appears. Nothing tells your eye where to look.

### Design Principles to Express
- **Depth through surface variation** — not just borders, but actual tonal difference between layers
- **Emerald as a character** — the brand color should appear meaningfully throughout the board, not just in the sidebar and one button
- **Column identity** — each stage column should feel like it has a distinct personality/color identity, not just a left border accent
- **Data density with breathing room** — information-dense but never claustrophobic
- **Premium darkness** — consider whether certain elements (column headers? the board background? section labels?) could be darker/richer rather than defaulting to light gray

### Specific Areas to Be Bold With

**Column headers:**
Current: white background, colored left border, uppercase label. Boring.
Consider: giving each column header a tinted background based on its stage color. Or a darker header background (near-black like the sidebar) with white text and a colored accent. The columns should feel like distinct zones, not identical white boxes with different colored stripes.

**Board background:**
Current: `#ECEEF2` — better than white but still feels like a spreadsheet.
Consider: a slightly richer, warmer tone. Or a very subtle geometric pattern (dots, lines) at low opacity. Or gradient from slightly darker at edges to lighter in center. Something that makes the board feel like a work surface rather than empty space.

**The pipeline title:**
Current: "Non-Surgical Pipeline ▾" in regular weight.
Make it more commanding — larger, bolder, darker. This is the name of the thing you're looking at. It should feel like a heading, not a dropdown label.

**Cards:**
The cards are the primary work objects. They should feel substantial — like something you'd want to pick up and move. Consider a slightly more pronounced shadow, a very subtle gradient on the card background (pure white at top, barely-perceptible warm tint at bottom), or a left accent bar on cards that matches their column color.

**Emerald usage:**
The brand color should appear: in column header accents, in the appointment chip icon, in the "+" Add Lead button, in the active filter state, in the rotting-free pipeline health indicator. Right now it's absent from the board almost entirely.
